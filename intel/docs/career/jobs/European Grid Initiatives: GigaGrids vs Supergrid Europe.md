# European Grid Initiatives: GigaGrids vs Supergrid Europe

## 1. GIGA (Green Industrial Grids Association)

**Website:** https://www.gigagrids.eu/
**Launched:** January 27, 2026 (Brussels)
**Type:** Industry association / advocacy group

### Mission
Acts as a unifying voice for **electricity users and grid technology providers** that play a central role in Europe's economy. Advocates for stronger, smarter and cleaner electricity grids.

### Key Advocacy Areas ("Our Asks")
1. **Demand-Side Coordination:** Stronger coordination with demand-side consumers to ensure grid build-out happens where most needed
2. **Third-Party Financing:** Mobilising financing for long-term affordable grid expansion
3. **Grid Enhancing Technologies (GETs):** Deploying technological solutions, non-wire solutions, and incentives
4. **Grid Access Reform:** Reforming grid access policy to allocate capacity based on project maturity, improve application processing, transparency

### Relevance to VIOTAS
- **Direct Relevance:** GIGA explicitly advocates for demand-side solutions and flexible connections
- **Policy Influence:** Working on European Grids Package reforms
- **Potential Partner/Stakeholder:** VIOTAS could engage with GIGA as a demand response provider

## 2. Supergrid Europe

**Website:** https://www.supergrideurope.eu/
**Launched:** 2025
**Type:** Non-profit advocacy organization
**Origin:** Launched by Irish think tank **Trifecta Energy**

### Mission
Promoting and supporting the acceleration and realisation of a **pan-European electricity Supergrid**. Working to ensure Europe's power system is fit for a decarbonised future, enabling:
- Energy independence
- Price stability
- Deep electrification through renewable electricity

### Key Focus Areas
- Coordinated EU grid policies
- Reforms of governance, grid planning, and market mechanisms
- Modern, interconnected grid infrastructure

### Relevance to VIOTAS
- **Irish Connection:** Founded by Irish think tank (Trifecta Energy)
- **Interconnector Focus:** Supergrid development will increase cross-border electricity flows, creating more need for flexible demand response
- **Long-Term Vision:** Aligns with 2030-2035 timeframe for European grid transformation
