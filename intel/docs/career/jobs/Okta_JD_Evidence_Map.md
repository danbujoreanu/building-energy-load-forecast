## SECTION 5: The One Genuine Gap — How to Handle It

### The Gap
You have never held a formal **"Privacy Ops"** or **"Vendor Procurement"** title. If asked directly — "have you worked in privacy operations?" or "what is your procurement experience?" — a defensive or vague answer will hurt you.

### What Is Actually True
You have operated **inside** these functions throughout your career:
- You made real data provenance and usage rights decisions at Meta (GDPR-governed environment)
- You built GDPR-compliant documentation for your own production AI system (5-source data provenance, full audit trail)
- You applied EU AI Act risk classification and impact assessment frameworks to a live production system
- You gatekept the quality and compliance standards for what entered the ML experiment portfolio
- You have never purchased third-party AI vendor contracts, but you have evaluated and selected external data providers, assessed their compliance, and documented your decisions

You did the work. You did not have the title.

### How to Handle It If Asked Directly

> *"I haven't held a Privacy Ops or Vendor Procurement title specifically. What I have done is operated inside those functions throughout my career — making real GDPR decisions at Meta in an environment where the consequences of getting it wrong were high, and most recently building full data provenance and EU AI Act compliance documentation for my own production AI system from scratch. The governance instinct is there; what I'd bring to Okta is that muscle applied with more structure and at larger scale."*

**What this does**:
- Acknowledges the gap directly — no hedging, no spin
- Immediately pivots to real evidence
- Frames the function as something you have done, not something you aspire to
- Ends with a forward-looking value proposition

**What to avoid**:
- "I've done similar things to privacy ops..." (too vague)
- Overstating what you have done (they will probe and it will unravel)
- Immediately pivoting to your strengths before you have acknowledged the gap (reads as evasive)
