# Prompt for Google Deep Research: VIOTAS Market & Competitive Intelligence Report (Multi-Horizon Analysis)

## 1. ROLE AND GOAL

You are a senior strategy consultant with deep expertise in the energy sector, digital transformation, and competitive strategy. Your goal is to produce a comprehensive, board-level market and competitive intelligence report for VIOTAS, a leading demand response and virtual power plant operator headquartered in Ireland. This report will serve as a foundational support document for a strategic AI initiative, analyzing the market across three distinct time horizons: **2026 (Present), 2030 (Medium-Term), and 2035 (Long-Term)**.

## 2. REPORT STRUCTURE & TONE

Produce a professional, well-structured report using formal business language. The report should be organized into the following sections:

1.  **Executive Summary:** A high-level overview of the key findings, opportunities, and strategic recommendations across the three time horizons.
2.  **European Energy Market Landscape (2026-2035):** Analysis of macro trends, headwinds, and tailwinds, with specific focus on:
    *   **2026:** Google's entry, EirGrid T-4 auctions, LEAP program.
    *   **2030:** North Sea offshore wind buildout, Celtic Interconnector.
    *   **2035:** Pan-European Supergrid, 300 GW wind target.
3.  **Competitive Analysis:** A detailed breakdown of the competitive landscape, including how competitors are positioned for each time horizon.
4.  **Strategic Opportunity Analysis:** Identification of strategic gaps and potential pathways for VIOTAS in the short, medium, and long term.
5.  **References:** A list of all sources cited.

## 3. CORE TASK & FRAMEWORKS

Conduct a deep analysis of the European demand response and VPP market, applying the following academic and strategic frameworks:

### A. Competitive Strategy (Grant & Porter Focus)

*   **Industry Analysis:** Apply **Porter's Five Forces** to the European demand response market. How does the threat of new entrants like **Google** change the industry structure?
*   **Sources of Competitive Advantage:** Based on **Robert M. Grant's "Contemporary Strategy Analysis,"** analyze how leading competitors are achieving competitive advantage. Is it through cost leadership, differentiation, or a focus strategy?
*   **Strategic Groups:** Map the Top 20 competitors into strategic groups based on their business models and technology stacks.

### B. Digital Transformation & Innovation (UCD Smurfit MBA Focus)

*   **Dynamic Capabilities (Teece, Pisano & Shuen):** Assess the industry's dynamism. How are leading firms sensing, seizing, and reconfiguring their assets to adapt to market changes like the **LEAP announcement**, the **North Sea Investment Pact**, and the development of the **European Supergrid**?
*   **Organizational Ambidexterity (O'Reilly & Tushman):** How are competitors balancing the need to **exploit** their existing business models (e.g., participating in 2026 T-4 auctions) with the need to **explore** new innovations for the 2030-2035 market (e.g., pan-European flexibility services)?
*   **Disruptive Innovation (Christensen):** Is **Google's vertical integration** (owning power generation) a disruptive threat to the aggregator business model?

### C. Operations & Agentic AI (Modern AI Strategy Focus)

*   **System 1 vs. System 2 Automation:** Drawing on the concepts from Kahneman and the IT Revolution article by Matt McLarty, analyze how competitors are likely using automation. Distinguish between:
    *   **System 1 (Operational Efficiency):** Use of tools like **Power Automate**, n8n, or custom scripts for deterministic, repeatable tasks.
    *   **System 2 (Market Innovation):** Use of agentic AI for complex, probabilistic tasks (e.g., real-time trading, predictive forecasting).
*   **"Automation that Works":** Ground your analysis in the reality that most enterprise AI is focused on practical, reliable automation rather than purely experimental R&D, referencing the MIT finding that 95% of AI pilots fail.

## 4. SCOPE OF ANALYSIS

### A. Competitor Identification

Identify and analyze the **Top 20 competitors** to VIOTAS in Ireland and Europe. Use the following list as a starting point and expand upon it. Categorize them into:

1.  **Direct Competitors (DR & VPPs):** Sympower, Next Kraftwerke, GridBeyond, Flexitricity, Enel X.
2.  **Smart Grid & Infrastructure Providers:** Landis+Gyr, EpiSensor, Schneider Electric, Siemens AG.
3.  **Specialized Flexibility & SaaS Solutions:** Enode, NovoGrid, Zenobē.
4.  **New Entrants:** Google.

### B. For each competitor, analyze:

*   **Stated Strategy & Business Model:** How do they make money? What is their value proposition?
*   **Technology Stack (Inferred):** What technologies are they likely using? Look for clues in job descriptions, press releases, and technical blogs. Do they mention specific AI platforms, cloud providers, or tools like Power Automate?
*   **AI Application Focus:** Is their AI focus on internal operational efficiency (System 1) or external market-facing innovation (System 2)?
*   **Positioning for 2030/2035:** How are they preparing for the long-term market changes?

## 5. KEY QUESTIONS TO ADDRESS

*   **Headwinds & Tailwinds (Multi-Horizon):** What are the major forces impacting the industry in 2026, 2030, and 2035?
*   **The "Sequoia vs. Bonsai" Dilemma:** Referencing the concept from Nicolas Darveau-Garneau, are competitors focused on narrow efficiency gains ("Bonsai") or are they building scalable platforms for profitable growth ("Sequoia")?
*   **The Agentic Coding Shift:** Based on the "2026 Agentic Coding Trends Report," how is the shift from traditional software development to agentic workflows likely to impact the speed of innovation in this sector?
*   **EirGrid Capacity Market:** Analyze the results of past **T-4 auctions** and the parameters for the upcoming **2029/2030 auction**. What are the implications for VIOTAS?
*   **European Grid Initiatives:** What are the strategic implications of **GigaGrids** and **Supergrid Europe** for VIOTAS's business model?
*   **Strategic Gaps:** Where are the uncontested market spaces or underserved customer needs in each time horizon?

## 6. OUTPUT FORMAT

Present the final output as a single, comprehensive markdown document. Use tables to compare competitors and frameworks. Use clear headings and subheadings to structure the report logically. Ensure all claims are supported by evidence and all sources are cited in a final "References" section.
