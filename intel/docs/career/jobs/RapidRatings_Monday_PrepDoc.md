**Story: S009 — The R&F Delivery Curve | Target: ~90 seconds, then invite probing**

**Opening line (say this first):**
> *"I owned the Reach & Frequency product support stream at Meta — R&F is the premium ad buying product for major brand advertisers, campaigns often worth over a million dollars."*

**Situation (15 sec):**
> *"The problem: R&F delivery follows Facebook's user availability, so it's non-linear. Advertisers had a flat daily report — and when delivery dipped mid-campaign, they panicked. **Task (10 sec):**
> *"My roadmap goal was to eliminate the information asymmetry. If I could externalise the internal delivery chart into Ads Manager, panic stops and cases stop."*

**Action (30 sec):**

### Q3 — "When did user behaviour cause you to change direction?"

**Story: S008 — The IDD Bypass Pivot | Target: ~75 seconds**

---

**Opening line:**
> *"The IDD adoption story is the clearest example I have — and the pivot completely changed how I think about user behaviour signals."*

**Context (15 sec):**
> *"I'd built IDD — a Sales-facing diagnostic tool for campaign delivery issues. Designed to let Sales resolve cases before they escalated to Tier 2. We launched it, trained Sales teams, gave them access."*

**The behaviour signal (15 sec):**
> *"Usage data told us Sales was still bypassing IDD and filing cases directly. Not because IDD was technically broken — they just defaulted to the familiar path. Issuing more training wasn't going to fix it. The behaviour was telling us something about the product experience, not about the users."*

**The pivot (20 sec):**
> *"I made the call to integrate IDD into the contact form itself. If you're filing a case, you now go through IDD first. If it resolves — done. If it doesn't — you continue filing, and the IDD data pre-populates your case, making it faster to resolve anyway. It wasn't a gate. It was a shortcut. We changed the experience instead of trying to change the user."*

**Impact and learning (15 sec):**
> *"Six thousand monthly active users. Roughly 1 in 4 resolved without filing — approximately 10% ticket deflection. The lesson: when users bypass your feature, they're not being difficult — they're telling you the experience isn't good enough yet."*
