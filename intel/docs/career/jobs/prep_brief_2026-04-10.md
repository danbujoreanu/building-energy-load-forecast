## The Three Questions He Will Ask (Versions Of)

### Q1 — "Tell me about yourself and why you're applying."

**What he's really asking:** Why a PhD at this stage? Is this genuine or a hedge while you job hunt?

**Your answer (90 seconds):**
> "My background is product and programme management — 15 years at Meta, eBay, PayPal. In 2024–25 I did something unusual: two full-time masters simultaneously, an MBA at UCD Smurfit and an MSc in AI at NCI. My thesis was on energy load forecasting — comparing 8 ML approaches across 45 buildings. The paper was published at AICS 2025 through Springer.
>
> Since then I've been building the production version — a residential energy forecasting app targeting the CRU June 2026 dynamic pricing mandate. It's live, it's showing €178/year savings, and I've built the full AI governance stack for it — Model Card, AI Impact Assessment under EU AI Act, Data Provenance, Data Lineage.
>
> The reason I'm applying now is that the research questions I'm hitting in production require the depth and compute access that independent work can't provide. And the Decarb-AI programme sits exactly at the intersection of where I already am — AI-optimised energy systems, Irish regulatory context, commercialisation pathway."

### Q2 — "What research question would you pursue?"

**What he's really asking:** Is your thinking original, or are you just enthusiastic about the topic?

**Your answer:**
> "The question I keep hitting in my production work is this: current ML approaches for residential energy optimisation assume either perfect demand elasticity or static user behaviour. Neither is true under dynamic pricing. When the CRU mandate goes live in June 2026, millions of Irish households will face real-time price signals for the first time — and the models that work for commercial buildings won't transfer cleanly.
>
> My research question would be: how do you design AI decision-support systems for residential heat pump scheduling and energy storage dispatch that are both accurate under dynamic pricing conditions AND transparent enough to meet EU AI Act requirements? The governance layer isn't optional — EIOPA and the Central Bank are already looking at AI in consumer financial decisions. Energy pricing is next.
>
> The commercialisation angle is already defined: Sparc Energy, which I'm in the process of incorporating. The PhD would be the research foundation for the product roadmap."

### Q3 — "You're 45 with 15 years of industry experience. Why not just build the company?"

**What he's really asking:** Are you committed to 4 years of research rigour, or will you leave when a corporate offer arrives?

**Your answer:**
> "Two reasons. First, I don't need a mortgage. That's not a flippant answer — it's a genuine constraint that doesn't apply. The stipend works for my situation.
>
> Second, and more importantly: the problems I'm trying to solve require academic infrastructure. ICHEC's HPC access, multi-institution data partnerships, peer review. You can't build a policy-relevant model on a residential energy dataset from one home. I need the programme to do the research properly. And honestly — I spent 15 years executing other people's visions. Four years building something that could actually influence national energy policy is worth more to me than another corporate role."
